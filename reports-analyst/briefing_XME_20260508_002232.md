# Alpha Briefing: XME Mining & Metals Portfolio
**Date:** May 8, 2026
**Focus:** Infrastructure, Data Centers, and Critical Mineral Independence

## Executive Summary
The XME Metals & Mining sector is entering a multi-year "super-cycle" driven by the delayed disbursement of IIJA infrastructure funds, a "white-hot" data center expansion, and aggressive U.S. trade protectionism. **Commercial Metals Company (CMC)** and **Warrior Met Coal (HCC)** represent the highest-conviction value-growth opportunities, while **Nucor (NUE)** is the premier play on digital infrastructure.

## Top High-Conviction Buys

### 1. Commercial Metals Company (CMC) | Score: 65.95%
*   **The Thesis:** CMC is no longer just a steelmaker; it is a comprehensive infrastructure solutions provider. The **$1.84B acquisition of Foley Products** has shifted the portfolio toward higher-margin precast concrete, perfectly timed for the peak of IIJA spending.
*   **Catalyst:** 60% of IIJA funds remain unspent, creating a "coiled spring" demand effect for late 2025/2026.
*   **Key Metric:** Trading at **10.29x Forward P/E**, a discount to its 10-year median, despite a projected **17% revenue rebound** in 2026.
*   **Target:** $80.00+ (from ~$60 range).

### 2. Warrior Met Coal (HCC) | Score: 56.20%
*   **The Thesis:** HCC is at a massive operational inflection point. The **Blue Creek Mine** ramp-up (longwall started Oct 2025, 8 months early) is transforming the company's production profile and cost structure.
*   **Catalyst:** Total capacity is increasing by **75%** to 14M short tons. Analysts project a **621% EPS increase** in 2026 as high-volume production meets stable ~$180-$220/t met coal prices.
*   **Key Metric:** DCF models suggest intrinsic value of **$144** (currently ~$90).
*   **Recommendation:** Aggressive Buy on production volume growth.

### 3. Nucor (NUE) | Score: 57.69%
*   **The Thesis:** The undisputed winner of the "Data Center Explosion." Through Nucor Data Systems (NDS), the company supplies **95% of the steel components** needed for AI data centers.
*   **Catalyst:** 50% steel import tariffs (implemented June 2025) have decimated foreign competition, dropping import market share to 14%.
*   **Key Metric:** Historic order backlogs (up 40% YoY). 21.30% Revenue Growth.

## Strategic "National Security" Bets

### MP Materials (MP) | Score: 53.15%
*   **Catalyst:** Transitioning from miner to manufacturer. **Commercial magnet shipments** begin late 2025.
*   **Alpha:** DoD price floor protection (~$110/kg for NdPr) provides a safety net against Chinese price dumping.

### USA Rare Earth (USAR) | Score: 55.86%
*   **Catalyst:** **Stillwater Magnet Facility** commissioning (Q1 2026). Strategic $1.6B government funding access makes it a "national strategic asset."
*   **Note:** High-risk, pre-revenue, but massive momentum (+200% in 2025).

## Macro Context & Catalysts
*   **Infrastructure:** IIJA disbursement hitting peak construction phase; bridge plates and rebar demand at record highs.
*   **Data Centers:** 3,500 tons of steel required per 250k sq. ft. AI-driven capacity expansion is "white hot."
*   **Tariffs:** 50% Section 232 tariffs providing a structural floor for domestic HRC prices at ~$850-$900/ton.

## Avoid List
*   **CLF (Cleveland-Cliffs):** 45.41% Score. Negative margins and higher sensitivity to the lagging automotive sector compared to CMC/NUE's infrastructure exposure.
*   **LEU (Centrus Energy):** 31.64% Score. Weak fundamental metrics compared to the steel/coal alpha.
