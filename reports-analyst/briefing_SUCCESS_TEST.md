## Deep Dive

### 1. Commercial Metals Company (CMC)
*   **The Thesis**: Despite a temporary earnings miss in Q2, CMC is the top-ranked asset due to its structural transformation. It is moving beyond commodity steel into "infrastructure solutions" via precast concrete acquisitions.
*   **Key Metrics**: 12% ROE, 2.38x Current Ratio (liquidity powerhouse).
*   **Catalyst**: Diversification into higher-margin segments like precast concrete (CP&P, Foley) provides a valuation floor and shields against steel price volatility.

### 2. Nucor Corporation (NUE)
*   **The Thesis**: The "King of Steel" continues to dominate through efficiency and pricing power. Domestic trade protections (tariffs) are effectively capping import competition.
*   **Key Metrics**: 21.3% Revenue Growth, 14.43x Forward P/E (attractive valuation for a market leader).
*   **Catalyst**: Recent $4B buyback authorization and record shipments in Q1 2026. Goldman Sachs price target raised to $260.

### 3. Steel Dynamics (STLD)
*   **The Thesis**: Best-in-class operational efficiency reflected in the highest ROE among the top steel group (15.25%).
*   **Key Metrics**: 15.25% ROE, 19.10% Revenue Growth.
*   **Catalyst**: Expansion into the aluminum market and the successful ramp-up of the Sinton mill are providing new growth vectors outside core steel.

## Macro Context: The "Reshoring" Supercycle
The metals sector is currently riding a "triple threat" of positive drivers:
1.  **Infrastructure Spend**: Continued rollout of federal infrastructure funds.
2.  **Trade Protection**: Declining import share (from 25% to 14%) due to aggressive tariff enforcement.
3.  **Supply Shocks**: Geopolitical tensions (Strait of Hormuz) keeping domestic HRC prices elevated above $1,000/ton.
