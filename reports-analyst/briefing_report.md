## Methodology and Logic Trace

### Step 1: Quantitative Filtering
The initial screen prioritized **Total Score (%)**, which aggregates 20+ metrics. I specifically looked for "Anomalies of Excellence"—stocks with multiple 100% Strength scores in fundamental efficiency (ROE, Margins) and growth.

### Step 2: Metric Prioritization
For the 2026 context, I prioritized the following hierarchy:
1.  **PEG Ratio & Forward P/E**: Essential to ensure "Alpha" isn't already fully priced in. (Selected ADBE for its deep valuation discount).
2.  **ROE & EBITDA Margin**: Indicators of structural moats and "AI Efficiency" (Selected APP for its 200%+ ROE).
3.  **Revenue Growth**: Essential for growth-style "Buy" stories (Selected META for its 33% scale-adjusted growth).

### Step 3: Qualitative Catalyst Correlation
I cross-referenced the top quantitative performers with real-world 2026 market developments:
*   **APP** was selected because its financial metrics (60% margins) were so high they required a specific catalyst explanation—found in the "AXON E-commerce" pivot.
*   **META** was selected because its PEG < 1.0 during a period of peak Capex suggests a massive "Efficiency" payout as that infrastructure matures.
*   **ADBE** was selected because its Forward P/E of 9.6x (per report) vs. its ROE of 58% indicates a significant market mispricing regarding AI's impact on its creative moat.

### Step 4: Final Conviction Weighting
Stocks like **TSLA** (Score 38%) were excluded due to poor margin strength (7%) and extreme valuation (360x Trailing P/E). The focus remained strictly on companies showing "The Trifecta": High Margins, Accelerated Growth, and Reasonable Valuations.
